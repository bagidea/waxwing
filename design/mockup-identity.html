waxwing LOCKED
A waxwing is a real crested bird named for the wax-red droplets on its wingtips → a built-in tie to WAX. The wallet's whole job is signing — your keys stay on your machine and the transaction takes flight. Short, ownable, mascot-ready, and steps out of Anchor's heavy maritime shadow.
2
Helm
Self-sovereignty made active: you steer your own ship. Where an anchor weighs you down, the helm puts you in control. 4 letters, strong — sits next to Anchor but one-ups the metaphor.
3
Seal
The most literal WAX play — you seal each transaction in wax. Warm, concrete, easy to say in any of our 14 languages. Slight risk: a common word.
4
Cardinal
Navigation's cardinal marks + a “cardinal rule” of trust + a compass direction. Premium, editorial feel. Longer, but distinctive and confident.
5
Quill
You sign with a quill, then press the wax seal. Craft & heritage tone, elegant — pairs beautifully with the seal motif if we lean artisanal.
